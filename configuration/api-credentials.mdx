Botky connects to the UChat Partner API using your Partner Token. Every workspace operation — listing clients, creating workspaces, changing plans, managing add-ons — is authenticated with this single token. Without it, no API calls can succeed, so connecting your token is the first thing you should do after installing the plugin.

## Get Your Partner Token

Before configuring Botky, retrieve your token from UChat:

1. Log in to your UChat account at [uchat.com.au](https://www.uchat.com.au).
2. Navigate to your **Partner** settings.
3. Locate and copy your **Partner API Token**.

## Save Your Token in Botky

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Botky dashboard">
    Navigate to the Botky panel — either through the WordPress admin area or the front-end page where you embedded the `[botky_panel]` shortcode.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Settings in the sidebar">
    Select the **Settings** tab from the left-hand sidebar navigation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste your Partner Token">
    In the **API Credentials** section, paste your Partner Token into the token field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Save">
    Press the **Save** button to store your token. Botky saves it securely and associates it with your WordPress account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the connection">
    Watch the sidebar connection indicator. Once your token is saved successfully, it turns green and displays **Connected**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Keep your Partner Token confidential. It grants full access to all your partner workspaces and client accounts. Never share it publicly, commit it to version control, or expose it in client-facing content.
</Warning>

## Connection Status Indicators

After saving your token, the sidebar shows a live status indicator:

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 🟢 **Connected** | Your token is saved and all workspace and client operations will authenticate successfully. |
| 🔴 **No API Key** | Your token is missing. All workspace and client operations will fail until you save a valid token. |

<Note>
  Each WordPress user stores their own API token independently. If multiple team members access the Botky dashboard on the same site, each person must open their own Settings tab and configure their own Partner Token.
</Note>

## Troubleshooting

**"No API Key" error appears after saving**

Ensure you pasted the full token without any leading or trailing spaces. Copy the token again directly from your UChat partner settings and re-paste it into the field before saving.

**Operations still fail after saving the token**

Verify your token is active and valid by checking your UChat partner account. A token that has been regenerated or revoked in UChat will cause all API calls to fail even if it appears saved in Botky.
