The **Clients** tab shows every user registered under your UChat partner account in a single, searchable directory. Use it to quickly look up a client's contact details, verify their workspace count, or confirm whether a specific person exists in your account before creating new resources for them.

## Client Table

The client list loads automatically when you switch to the Clients tab. Each row represents one client and displays the following columns:

| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| **ID** | The unique numeric identifier assigned to the client by UChat. |
| **Client** | The client's full name as registered in UChat. |
| **Contact** | The client's email address and phone number. |
| **Teams** | The workspace (team) names associated with this client. |
| **Total** | The total number of workspaces this client belongs to. |

---

## Searching Clients

Type a name or email address into the search field in the header bar, then press **Enter**. The table reloads and displays only the clients that match your query across all pages — you do not need to navigate to a specific page first.

To clear the search and return to the full list, delete the text in the search field and press **Enter** again, or click the **Refresh** button (circular arrow icon) in the header.

---

## Pagination

Use the **Prev** and **Next** buttons at the bottom of the table to move between pages. The current page number and total page count are displayed next to the pagination controls so you always know where you are in the full list.

<Info>
The client list is read-only. Clients are created automatically by UChat when you provision a workspace for them through the **Workspaces** tab — there is no separate "add client" action in Botky. To register a new client, [create a workspace](/dashboard/workspaces#creating-a-workspace) and fill in their details in the creation form.
</Info>

<Tip>
If you know a client's workspace ID, switch to the **Workspaces** tab and type the numeric ID directly into the search field. This takes you straight to that workspace without needing to find the client first.
</Tip>
